A vulnerability scanner is a valuable investment. CSTOOL.io is not meant to replace local scanning. It is supposed to help in those cases where you are not able to do it quickly. In some cases you are not able to actively run a host- or network-based scanner at all. This may be the case when working with isolated assets or relying on external service providers.

CSTOOL.io scans for publicly known vulnerabilities.

CSTOOL.io continuously scans official sources for published vulnerabilities in known software and hardware products. You simply add the products that you are using to your inventory. Know your weaknesses and show that without proper patch management the problem will not go away. Start with the components that you know that you are using. Add components that you know that your service providers are using. Improve your inventory as you become more aware of your infrastructure over time.

Don't know where to start? Try this:

With AttackSrfc you don't have to setup a host- or network-based scanner to get your vulnerability- and patch management-processes going.

The CPE inventory and CVE search allows you to get started immediately.

CSTOOL.io can even be useful if you are already using another scanning tool. You can use the report from your scanner or asset discovery in AttackSrfc to improve your results! Simply add the identified CPEs to your CSTOOL.io inventory.

A security scan of a system will give you a detailed view of the vulnerabilities present on that particular system. But what about systems you cannot scan, such as those of vendors, partners or SaaS solutions? You can use AttackSrfc to get a quick view of publicly known vulnerabilities to start a qualified discussion with your third parties about their mitigation workflow.
